# Routing and Handoff

Routing and handoff define how the workflow exits the ideal path. In a production system, these
paths are not edge cases. They are part of the design surface and should be explicit from the
start.

## Routing should be intentional

Use routing to direct calls based on known context, caller intent, service line, urgency, business
hours, or unresolved ambiguity. The goal is to shorten time to the right outcome, not simply to
keep the caller inside automation.

## Handoff should preserve context

When the workflow hands off to a person or another system, the useful context from the interaction
should move with it where possible. Good handoff design reduces repetition for both the caller and
the receiving team.

## Define handoff triggers up front

- policy or compliance boundaries
- missing or conflicting context
- repeated failure to confirm intent
- high-value or high-risk interactions
- caller preference for a human path
