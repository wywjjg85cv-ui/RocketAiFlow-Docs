# Inbound Workflows

Inbound workflows begin when the caller decides to engage. That changes the design problem. The
workflow must quickly understand intent, confirm context, and route the call toward resolution or
the right next owner.

## Common inbound use cases

- first-line intake and qualification
- support triage and routing
- appointment or service coordination
- order, account, or request status handling
- structured information capture before human handoff

## Design priorities

- identify intent early without over-questioning
- confirm relevant account or case context before taking action
- route confidently when the call belongs elsewhere
- keep handoff smooth when the caller already provided information

## What usually makes inbound automation credible

Inbound automation feels reliable when it reduces friction, not when it tries to contain every
call. In practice, that means narrow decision trees, fast access to business context, and explicit
handoff rules for exceptions, urgency, or policy-sensitive scenarios.
