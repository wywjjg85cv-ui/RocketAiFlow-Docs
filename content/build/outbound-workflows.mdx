# Outbound Workflows

Outbound workflows start from a task, event, or queue rather than from caller intent. That makes
pre-call targeting, pacing, qualification logic, and outcome capture especially important.

## Where outbound fits well

- reminders and confirmations
- lead or prospect qualification
- payment or account follow-up
- service follow-through and re-engagement
- status collection after a prior event or request

## Design priorities

- start from a clearly defined business trigger
- know what outcome should be captured from the call
- handle failed contact, deferral, or disinterest cleanly
- define when the workflow should create a task for human follow-up

## Operational notes

Outbound systems should be reviewed not only for conversation quality but also for list hygiene,
contact policy, retry logic, and task ownership. The workflow is only useful if the resulting
outcomes are dependable enough to feed the next operational step.
