# Core Concepts

RocketAiFlow should be understood as a workflow system for business phone operations, not just a
conversation engine. The call matters, but the surrounding logic, data access, routing, and
operational controls matter just as much.

## The main building blocks

- **Workflow**: the business process being automated, including entry conditions and desired
  outcomes.
- **Context**: the data available during the call, such as customer records, task state, or
  scheduling information.
- **Decision logic**: the rules that determine whether the workflow continues, changes path, or
  hands off.
- **Actions**: the work the system performs, such as lookups, updates, notifications, or callback
  creation.
- **Handoff**: the defined path for unresolved, risky, or out-of-scope interactions.

## Why this matters

Good Voice AI outcomes depend on more than prompt quality. A workflow usually fails because the
system lacks context, makes the wrong routing choice, or has no safe recovery path when the call
does not match the ideal flow.

## Recommended modeling approach

Start with the business objective and the operational boundary:

1. Define what the workflow is supposed to resolve.
2. Define what data is needed to resolve it.
3. Define what conditions require escalation.
4. Define what system updates or side effects should happen after the call.

This keeps the design grounded in operational reality instead of turning the call into an
unbounded conversational surface.
