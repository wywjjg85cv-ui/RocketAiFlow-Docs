# Traces Overview

Where tracing is enabled in the deployment, traces complement dashboards and logs by showing how a
request or workflow path moved across services and integrations.

## Why traces matter

Dashboards are strong at showing aggregate behavior. Logs are strong at showing events. Traces are
useful when teams need path-level visibility across a distributed workflow.

## When to use traces

Use traces when:

- a dashboard confirms the symptom but not the path
- logs show an event but not the full sequence around it
- the investigation crosses application, API, webhook, or supporting service boundaries

## Practical role in RocketAiFlow

For RocketAiFlow, traces are best treated as a complement to:

- call records
- pre-provisioned Grafana dashboards
- logs and telephony review

They are not a replacement for the core monitoring foundation. They help when teams need more
precise path-level investigation.
