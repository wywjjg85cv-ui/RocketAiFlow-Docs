# Workflow Optimization

Optimization should focus on the highest-friction parts of the workflow first. In production, the
best improvements usually come from better routing, clearer task boundaries, improved context
access, or stronger handoff rules rather than from making the conversation more elaborate.

## Where to look first

- repeated escalation patterns
- unresolved intents that should become explicit branches
- low-value questions that can be removed
- missing business context that forces unnecessary fallback

## Optimization rule of thumb

Make the workflow simpler before you make it broader. A narrow flow that resolves reliably is more
valuable than a wide flow that appears capable but behaves unpredictably.
