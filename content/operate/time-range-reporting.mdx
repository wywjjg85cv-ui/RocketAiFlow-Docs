# Time-Range Reporting

Selected time windows matter because one dashboard moment rarely tells the full story. Teams need
to compare the current operating state with a recent baseline, a previous campaign window, or a
known peak period.

## What to review by time range

Common reporting views include:

- total call volume
- answered calls, no answer, busy calls, failed calls, and congestion events
- campaign activity over time
- active calls versus configured limits over time
- average talk time over time
- average ring time over time
- outcome distribution by campaign during the selected window

## Practical comparisons

Useful comparisons include:

- today versus the last business day
- morning versus afternoon pacing
- before and after a prompt or routing change
- before and after a provider, endpoint, or trunk event
- one campaign versus another campaign during the same period

## Why this helps

Time-range reporting helps teams separate a temporary anomaly from a real operating shift. It also
supports better conversations between operations, technical leads, and business owners.
