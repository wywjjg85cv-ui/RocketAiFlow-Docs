# Call Records

Call Records is the main validation surface after testing or running real calls.

Use it to confirm whether the workflow behaved the way you expected.

## What To Check First

After a test call, review:

- call status
- timestamps
- outcome
- transcript or recording data if available

These are the fastest indicators of whether the agent, route, campaign, and trunk were aligned
correctly.

## How To Use Call Records Operationally

Call Records helps you answer practical questions such as:

- did the call start and end as expected
- did the correct route or campaign appear to run
- did the conversation lead to transfer, hangup, or another intended outcome
- do you need to adjust prompt, routing, contacts, or trunk configuration

## A Good First Review Habit

For the first implementation, review call records after every controlled test. This makes it much
easier to see which setup change caused which result.

## Common Signals To Watch

- repeated failed calls
- missing expected outcomes
- transfer attempts that do not complete
- unexpected timing or abrupt call endings
