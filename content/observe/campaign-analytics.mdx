# Campaign Analytics

Campaign analytics should help teams answer a simple question: is the workflow behaving the way we
intended while it is live?

## Core campaign views

For outbound workflows, campaign analytics often centers on:

- campaign active state
- dialer status
- total calls
- active calls
- maximum active calls versus current active load
- calls per second or dialing pace
- outcome distribution across campaigns
- average talk time
- average ring time

## Questions teams should be able to answer

- Which campaigns are active right now?
- Are campaigns pacing within the intended operating limits?
- Are active calls staying within the intended campaign limit?
- Are answered, no answer, busy, failed, or congestion outcomes shifting?
- Is one campaign behaving differently from the rest?
- Did performance change after a prompt, data, or routing change?

## Campaign execution control

Campaign analytics is also where teams confirm whether execution settings are doing the right job.

Review:

- calls per second as the live pacing signal
- maximum active calls per campaign as the live concurrency guardrail
- outcome shifts after pacing or concurrency changes
- talk time and ring time after the campaign completes

This helps teams control dialer pressure and protect both campaign quality and infrastructure
readiness.

## How to read outcome distribution

Outcome distribution is more useful than raw volume alone. It helps teams see whether a campaign is
productive, stalled, or being affected by telephony, data quality, or timing issues.

Look for:

- unusually high no answer activity
- spikes in busy calls
- failed or congestion events that suggest infrastructure or provider issues
- talk time patterns that look too short or too long for the intended workflow

## Single campaign drill-down

Teams should be able to move from the aggregate view into one campaign without losing the operating
context. That drill-down is how operators decide whether one campaign is healthy, stalled, or
creating telephony pressure on its own.

## Practical review loop

1. Check campaign active state and dialing pace.
2. Compare active calls with the configured campaign limit.
3. Review total outcomes and active call load.
4. Compare current behavior with a recent baseline.
5. Investigate whether the issue belongs to workflow logic, data quality, telephony state, or
   execution settings.
