# Quickstart

Use this path when you want to understand the first practical setup motion for RocketAiFlow
without dropping into provider-specific implementation detail too early.

## What you need before you start

- A clear phone workflow to automate, not just a general interest in Voice AI.
- A caller journey with explicit success, failure, and escalation outcomes.
- Access to the business systems that provide context or receive results.
- A view on who will own deployment, monitoring, and workflow iteration after launch.

<Callout type="warning">
  Quickstart should end with a validated rollout plan, not just a working demo. Real readiness
  depends on routing logic, business-system fit, and operational ownership.
</Callout>

## Recommended first path

<Steps>
### Choose your deployment model

Decide whether you need on-premise control, cloud delivery speed, or a phased approach. This
choice affects networking, access, rollout speed, and who carries operational responsibility.

### Define your workflow

Write down the call objective, the inputs needed at runtime, the decision points, and the fallback
path when the workflow should transfer or stop. Keep the first workflow narrow enough to validate
cleanly.

### Connect systems and providers

Map the required integration layers: telephony, speech recognition, voice output, model logic, and
the internal systems that provide context or receive outcomes. Treat each category as a design
decision rather than a fixed vendor dependency.

### Test and validate

Run scenario-based validation against normal calls, ambiguous calls, failed lookups, and handoff
conditions. The goal is to confirm both conversational behavior and system-level reliability.

### Launch and monitor

Launch with logging, escalation expectations, and a review loop already defined. Early operations
should focus on containment quality, exception patterns, and system integration reliability.
</Steps>

## A good first implementation usually includes

- One clearly bounded workflow with measurable outcomes.
- One telephony path and one business-system integration path.
- A documented handoff rule for unresolved calls.
- A small review loop for transcripts, logs, and operational exceptions.
