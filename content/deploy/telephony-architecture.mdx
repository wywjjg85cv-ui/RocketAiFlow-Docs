# Telephony Architecture

RocketAiFlow is designed for provider flexibility and built for real SIP and PBX environments.

This is not a claim of universal compatibility or zero-configuration interoperability. It is a
practical telephony architecture built to fit different calling environments through deployment
planning, trunk-based setup, and validation during implementation.

## What the telephony layer does

The telephony layer connects RocketAiFlow workflows to the real calling environment used for:

- inbound call handling
- outbound campaign execution
- transfer behavior
- endpoint and trunk state visibility

Without a clear telephony layer, agents, prompts, and campaigns can look correct while the live
calling path still fails.

## Why Asterisk is used

RocketAiFlow uses an Asterisk-based telephony layer. The telephony architecture is built on
Asterisk-based foundations because that supports practical telephony control, trunk-based
connectivity, and fit with real SIP / PBX environments.

In practical terms, Asterisk contributes:

- call control for inbound and outbound flow execution
- support for SIP / PBX environment fit
- provider flexibility through trunk-based configuration
- a telephony control plane that can be monitored and troubleshot in real deployments

## How this supports provider flexibility

RocketAiFlow is designed to fit different SIP / PBX environments rather than one rigid provider
assumption.

Safe guidance for buyers and implementers:

- provider choice depends on deployment and configuration
- telephony interoperability should be validated during setup
- provider compatibility should be tested against the real environment in use

This gives teams flexibility without making misleading claims about automatic compatibility with
every vendor or provider.

## SIP / PBX environment fit

RocketAiFlow is built for real SIP and PBX environments, which matters when the platform has to
fit into existing business communication infrastructure.

Teams typically evaluate:

- what SIP or PBX environment is already in place
- how inbound and outbound paths should enter that environment
- how transfer destinations should be represented
- how operational teams will monitor endpoint and trunk behavior

## Trunk-based connectivity

Trunk configuration is where the flexible telephony architecture becomes concrete.

This is the layer that ties:

- the workflow model in RocketAiFlow
- the provider or PBX environment
- the inbound and outbound call path

That is why trunk setup appears early in the implementation and troubleshooting path.

## Deployment considerations

Telephony fit should be reviewed as part of deployment planning, not after launch.

Dedicated is the managed, isolated single-tenant model. Self-Hosted is the customer-controlled
model for AWS, GCP, or customer data centers.

Validate:

- whether Dedicated or Self-Hosted is the right fit for the support and policy model
- whether the SIP / PBX environment matches the chosen deployment model
- whether provider choice is appropriate for the environment
- whether inbound, outbound, and transfer paths are all covered
- whether monitoring and troubleshooting access exists for the operators who will support the system

## Monitoring and troubleshooting connections

Telephony architecture is tightly connected to operations.

Teams should expect to review:

- endpoint and trunk monitoring
- Asterisk dashboards and logs
- call records during workflow validation
- traces where workflow actions cross multiple services

The pre-provisioned dashboards are especially useful here because they provide day-one visibility
into the telephony layer without forcing teams to build the initial views from scratch.
