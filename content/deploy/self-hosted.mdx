# Self-Hosted

Self-Hosted deploys on your own infrastructure or cloud instance to meet strict internal policies
or sovereignty requirements. It is built for teams with existing DevOps resources and can fit AWS,
GCP, or customer data-center environments.

## Why teams choose this model

Common reasons include:

- existing telephony systems already live inside the same environment
- API actions must stay close to internal systems
- operational teams need direct control over runtime, network, and observability layers
- internal policies require the platform to run inside infrastructure the customer manages
- the team already has DevOps resources for deployment, incident review, and day-two operations

## What to validate

Before rollout, confirm:

- telephony and trunk access are aligned with the environment
- provider choice has been validated against the real SIP / PBX environment
- API actions can reach the internal systems they depend on
- logs, traces, and dashboards are accessible to the support team
- host, container, and database monitoring are ready before live launch
- the team is prepared to own runtime operations and upgrade planning

## What to watch during evaluation

The most common risk is assuming that customer control alone guarantees operational clarity.

It does not. Teams still need:

- a narrow first workflow
- clear ownership for incident review
- ready observability
- a controlled rollout path
- enough DevOps capacity for the chosen support model
