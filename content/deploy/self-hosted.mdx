# Customer Environment

A Customer Environment pilot aligns RocketAiFlow with the customer's existing technical and
operational context.

Use this model when telephony, CRM, helpdesk, calendar, internal APIs, data policy, access control,
or monitoring requirements need to be evaluated inside a customer-defined perimeter.

## Why teams choose this model

Common reasons include:

- existing SIP / PBX systems or provider relationships must be part of the pilot
- API actions must reach internal systems safely
- contact data, transcripts, recordings, and call history must follow customer policy
- operations teams need visibility into dashboards, logs, traces, and call records
- the pilot needs to validate real workflow behavior, not only a generic demo

## What to validate

Before live testing, confirm:

- telephony and trunk access are aligned with the customer environment
- provider choice has been validated against the real SIP / PBX setup
- callback, scheduled call, priority, and pacing behavior match campaign needs
- API actions can reach the systems they depend on
- recordings and transcripts are enabled only where operationally and legally appropriate
- dashboards, logs, traces, and call history are accessible to the team that will review the pilot
- runtime responsibilities and support paths are clear

## What to watch during evaluation

The most common risk is assuming that customer control alone guarantees operational clarity.

It does not. Teams still need:

- a narrow first workflow
- clear ownership for issue review
- ready observability
- a controlled rollout path
- enough operational capacity to investigate call quality, telephony behavior, API issues, and list quality
