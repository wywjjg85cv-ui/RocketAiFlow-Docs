# Guided Pilot Setup

Guided Pilot Setup is the recommended early-access model for teams validating RocketAiFlow on a
real phone workflow.

The goal is not to start from a generic installation. The goal is to define one measurable pilot:
one campaign or inbound path, one agent, one telephony route, one contact/data model, one set of API
actions, and one operational review loop.

## Why teams choose this model

Common reasons include:

- validating AI outbound campaigns before scaling list size or dialer pressure
- testing controlled inbound AI for repetitive calls or overflow
- proving callback, scheduling, priority, and pacing behavior on real contacts
- reviewing transcripts, recordings when enabled, call timing, and outcomes after each interaction
- aligning monitoring, logs, traces, and call history before broader rollout
- reducing setup ambiguity while the product is in early access

## What to define before launch

Before the pilot starts, confirm:

- the workflow objective and success metrics
- the contact list, priority model, callback rules, and scheduled contact behavior
- the agent prompt and function/API action set
- telephony path, trunk setup, provider assumptions, and transfer behavior
- whether transcripts and recordings are enabled
- data saved after each call and who can access it
- calls-per-second and maximum active-call limits for the first run
- where operators review live dashboards, logs, traces, and call records

## Where teams usually get into trouble

Guided pilots often fail operationally when:

- the first workflow is too broad
- the team tests volume before validating call quality and call review
- callback and scheduling behavior are not defined clearly
- recording or transcription expectations are not agreed before launch
- monitoring is treated as a later step instead of part of the pilot
- API dependencies are treated as background details
